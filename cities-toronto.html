Over 1 million South Asians in Toronto create the world's second-largest Diwali celebration outside Asia. Yonge-Dundas Square festivals, historic temples, Little India culture, and vibrant multicultural celebration.

Toronto is Canada's multicultural hub and home to the world's second-largest Diwali celebration outside Asia. With over 1 million South Asians (Indians, Pakistanis, Bangladeshis, Sri Lankans, Afghans), Toronto pulses with South Asian culture year-round â€” and especially during Diwali.

Yonge-Dundas Square Festival

The Main Event

Toronto's official Diwali festival at Yonge-Dundas Square is one of Canada's largest celebrations. Usually held on a Saturday closest to Diwali, it draws 80,000-100,000 people.

What to Expect

  • Performances: Bollywood, classical Indian, contemporary fusion dance
  • Music: Live bands, DJs, traditional and modern
  • Food: Street food, restaurants, desserts
  • Shopping: Vendors selling clothing, jewelry, crafts
  • Activities: Kids' activities, henna, face painting
  • Atmosphere: Incredibly welcoming, diverse, family-friendly

Practical Information

  • Date: Usually Saturday of Diwali weekend (Nov 7, 2026)
  • Time: 2 PM - 9 PM typically
  • Entry: FREE
  • Location: Yonge-Dundas Square (downtown Toronto)
  • Transport: Subway access (Dundas/Yonge station)

Tips

  • Arrive early for best experience (before 3 PM)
  • Bring layers (November Toronto is cold, 5-10Â°C)
  • Cash for vendors helpful
  • Plan 3-4 hours for festival

Temples & Spiritual Sites

Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple

One of Toronto's oldest Hindu temples. Significant Diwali celebrations with lighting displays, prayers, and community gathering.

  • Location: Downtown Toronto
  • Special Diwali hours and ceremonies

Hare Krishna Temple (ISKCON Toronto)

Large Krishna temple with active community. Diwali is celebrated with elaborated ceremonies and vegetarian feast.

Gurdwaras (Sikh Temples)

Multiple Sikh gurdwaras throughout Toronto celebrate Diwali/Bandi Chhor Divas with langar (community meals) and ceremonies.

Mosques & Other Temples

Toronto's diverse South Asian communities all celebrate. Jain temples, Buddhist centers, and others hold special Diwali events.
