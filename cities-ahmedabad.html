Bestu Varas â€” The Gujarati New Year

What is Bestu Varas?

Bestu Varas literally means "new year" in Gujarati. According to the tradition, the Gujarati New Year begins at the moment of the new moon (Amavasya) on Diwali day, specifically at dawn.

The day is dedicated to fresh starts, personal renewal, and new ventures. Gujarati merchants traditionally mark this day as the start of their new financial year. It's both a spiritual and practical celebration of new beginning.

How It Differs From Regular Diwali

  • Timing: Bestu Varas has a specific moment of beginning (dawn on Amavasya)
  • Significance: Personal and business renewal, not just good vs. evil triumph
  • Celebration: New clothes, new accounts opened, fresh starts
  • Traditions: Unique Gujarati customs and rituals

The Moment of Bestu Varas

On November 9, 2026 at dawn, the Gujarati New Year officially begins. Many families perform special pujas at this moment. Businessmen open new account books. It's a powerful moment of collective renewal.

Rangoli â€” Art Form of Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad's Rangoli Tradition

Ahmedabad is famous for the most elaborate, intricate rangoli designs in all of India. Rangoli-making here is elevated to an art form â€” using rice flour, colors, flowers, and sometimes even gold and silver leaf.

Traditional Gujarati rangoli follows specific geometric and symbolic patterns. Each design tells a story or conveys a blessing. During Diwali, entire neighborhoods transform into galleries of these colorful creations.

Types of Rangoli

  • Geometric: Precise mathematical patterns, incredibly detailed
  • Floral: Using real flowers combined with colored powders
  • Mythological: Depicting gods, goddesses, and sacred stories
  • Contemporary: Modern designs blending tradition with creativity

Rangoli Walks

During Diwali, walk through residential areas like Thaltej, Bodakdev, or Sabarmati Riverfront to see hundreds of rangoli designs. The concentration and quality of artistry is unmatched elsewhere in India.

Kite Festival Connection

Diwali & Kites

While the famous Ahmedabad kite festival (Uttarayan) happens in January, Diwali kite flying is also traditional in Gujarat. You may see kites in the sky during Diwali celebrations, particularly on Nov 9 when Bestu Varas begins.

The connection: Both celebrations mark new beginnings â€” Uttarayan marks the sun's journey northward; Diwali marks the New Year.

Gujarati Diwali Cuisine

Traditional Sweets

  • Gujhiya: Semolina pastry filled with khoa (milk fudge) and dry fruits â€” the quintessential Gujarati Diwali sweet
  • Fafda with Jalebi: Gram flour snack paired with orange syrup â€” traditional breakfast on Diwali day
  • Badam Pak: Almond fudge with ghee
  • Chikhalwali: Gram flour and jaggery sweet
  • Malpua: Sweet pancakes with condensed milk

Savory Specialties

  • Mohanthal: Gram flour pudding (savory or sweet)
  • Dhokla: Steamed cake made from gram flour â€” savory and light
  • Khichiyu: Traditional rice and lentil dish

Where to Eat

For Traditional: Visit local sweet shops (misthan mandap) in Old City. Try family-run restaurants for authentic Gujarati thali.

For Modern Fusion: Sabarmati Riverfront has trendy restaurants offering Gujarati cuisine with contemporary presentation.

Ahmedabad Diwali FAQ

What exactly is Bestu Varas?

The Gujarati New Year that begins at the moment of the new moon (Amavasya) on Diwali day. It's when the New Year officially starts in Gujarat.

Can I participate in Bestu Varas celebrations?

Yes, if you're with a host family or invited to a celebration. Visitors are warmly welcomed. If staying alone, ask your hotel or guides for community celebrations open to visitors.

When is the best time to see rangoli?

Nov 6-9, with peak activity on Nov 8-9. Walk early morning (5-8 AM) to see fresh rangoli before they're disturbed. Evening (5-8 PM) offers beautiful lighting for photography.

What should I wear?

Bright colors essential. If you want to fully embrace the tradition, rent a Chaniya Choli (women) or Dhoti-Kurta (men) for Diwali day.

How organized is the city?

Very well-organized. Ahmedabad is planned, has good infrastructure, and celebrations are organized. Unlike chaotic Delhi, everything runs smoothly here.

Is Gujarati food very spicy?

Gujarati cuisine is flavorful but not intensely spicy like North India. It's balanced, sweet, savory, and nutritious. Very family-friendly.
