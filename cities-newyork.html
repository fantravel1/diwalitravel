Diwali in New York/New Jersey

America's Largest Celebration

2+ million South Asians make NYC/New Jersey the epicenter of American Diwali. Times Square celebrations, Jackson Heights vibrancy, world-class temples, and America's most dynamic South Asian culture.

Diwali in NYC/New Jersey â€” An Overview

New York City and New Jersey together form America's South Asian capital. With over 2 million people of Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi, and Sri Lankan heritage, the NY/NJ region celebrates Diwali at a scale unmatched in the United States.

NYC brings international sophistication and Times Square visibility. New Jersey (particularly North Jersey: Piscataway, Edison, Jersey City) provides authentic community culture. Together, they offer the complete American Diwali experience.

When to Visit

Diwali 2026: November 6-10, 2026

Best Time: Arrive Nov 2, stay through Nov 12

Why NYC/NJ for Diwali:

  • Largest South Asian population in USA (2+ million)
  • Times Square festival (unique American-scale celebration)
  • World-class infrastructure and amenities
  • Excellent temples and spiritual centers
  • Outstanding Indian restaurants and food scene
  • International gateway (easy to add other US cities)
  • English-speaking, completely comfortable for travelers
  • Blend of authentic community and modern cosmopolitanism

Times Square Diwali Celebration

The Main Event

Times Square hosts one of America's most spectacular Diwali celebrations, recognized by Guinness World Records as one of the largest outdoor Diwali events outside Asia.

What to Expect

  • Scale: 50,000+ people, Times Square transforms
  • Performances: Bollywood, classical Indian, contemporary fusion
  • Music: Live bands, DJs, Indian and fusion music
  • Food: Indian restaurants present, street vendors
  • Activities: Henna, crafts, kids' activities
  • Celebrity presence: Bollywood stars often attend

Practical Information

  • Date: Usually Saturday of Diwali weekend (Nov 7, 2026)
  • Time: 11 AM - 8 PM typically
  • Entry: FREE
  • Location: Times Square (central Manhattan)
  • Transit: Multiple subway lines serve this area

Tips

  • Arrive very early (before 12 PM) for crowd management
  • Bring cash for vendors
  • Stay hydrated (even November, crowds can be overwhelming)
  • Plan 4-5 hours for full experience

Temples & Spiritual Centers

New York City

Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple (Manhattan) - Historic, active community, special Diwali ceremonies

Ganesha Temple (Flushing, Queens) - Major temple with large community, elaborate Diwali celebrations

Islamic Cultural Center & Other Spaces - Diverse South Asian religious communities

New Jersey Temples (More Authentic Community)

Sri Venkateswara Temple (Piscataway) - One of America's largest Hindu temples. Hosts elaborate Diwali celebrations with thousands in attendance. Massive Diwali festival feel.

Sri Guru Nanak Temple (Jersey City) - Sikh gurdwara with active community and Diwali/Bandi Chhor Divas celebrations

Vishnu Temple (Monmouth Junction) - Another major temple with significant community

Why NJ Temples Matter

  • More authentic community-focused celebrations
  • Easier access to local experience
  • Often more space for participation
  • Larger gatherings and communal dinners

NYC's most vibrant and diverse South Asian neighborhood. Incredibly multicultural with strong Indian, Pakistani, and Bangladeshi communities.

Historic Indian neighborhood, now more upscale but still Indian restaurants and shops.

America's largest concentration of South Asians outside major cities. Very authentic, least touristy, most authentic cultural experience.

  • Authentic restaurants and markets

  • Less for tourists, more for experience
