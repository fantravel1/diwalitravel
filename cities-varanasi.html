Dev Deepawali â€” The Festival of the Gods

What is Dev Deepawali?

Dev Deepawali is celebrated on the full moon (Kartik Purnima) following Diwali â€” typically 15 days after the main Diwali day. In 2026, Dev Deepawali will be on November 23.

According to Hindu mythology, on this day, the gods themselves (devtas) descended to earth and lit sacred fires along the Ganges ghats. Varanasi celebrates this divine descent with millions of oil lamps â€” a reenactment of the gods' lighting ceremony.

The Experience

Millions of pilgrims and tourists gather on the ghats of Varanasi to light diyas and witness the Ganges transform into a river of light. The experience is profoundly moving â€” the chanting of mantras, the fragrance of incense, the soft glow of oil lamps, and the collective spiritual energy create something transcendent.

When to Visit Varanasi

Main Diwali (Nov 6-10): The five-day Diwali festival proper, with temple ceremonies and city-wide celebrations.

Dev Deepawali (Nov 23): The ultimate experience â€” millions of diyas on the Ganges ghats. This is when Varanasi's Diwali truly peaks.

Best Time: If possible, visit for both Main Diwali (Nov 8) and Dev Deepawali (Nov 23). If you can only choose one, Dev Deepawali is the must-see.

Must-See Experiences

Dev Deepawali on the Ganges Ghats

This is the defining experience. On Dev Deepawali night, arrive at the ghats in the late afternoon. As dusk falls, pilgrims begin lighting diyas. By evening, the ghats are ablaze with millions of oil lamps, their reflections dancing on the Ganges.

Practical Tips:

  • Arrive early (by 4 PM) to secure a good viewing spot.
  • Bring water, snacks, and a light shawl.
  • The event lasts several hours â€” stay patient and respectful.
  • Photography is allowed but be considerate of those praying.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

One of the holiest temples in Hinduism. During Diwali, the temple is decorated and packed with pilgrims. Early morning visits are less crowded.

Ghat Aarti Ceremonies

The daily Aarti (fire ritual) at the ghats is spectacular year-round, but during Diwali, it's even more elaborate. Visit in the early morning or evening.

Manikarnika Ghat

The most sacred ghat where cremations happen. It may seem somber, but it's a powerful place representing the Hindu cycle of life, death, and rebirth â€” central to Diwali's spiritual meaning.

Sarnath

A Buddhist pilgrimage site 12 km from Varanasi where Buddha gave his first sermon. Visit the ancient stupas and temples during Diwali.

Where to Stay in Varanasi

Ghat Area

Pros: Immersive experience, close to Dev Deepawali action, authentic atmosphere.

Cons: Crowded, can be noisy, basic amenities, congested lanes.

Cantonment/Sigra

Pros: Cleaner, better hotels, easier transportation, quieter.

Cons: Less authentic, 20-30 min from ghats.

Assi Ghat Area

Pros: Balance of authenticity and comfort, less crowded than main ghats.

Booking Tips: Book 3-4 months in advance for Diwali period. During Dev Deepawali, hotels triple their rates and sell out completely.

Diwali Rituals & Spiritual Practices

Lighting Your Own Diya

Buy a clay diya and oil from local vendors. Light it on Dev Deepawali night and float it on the Ganges or place it on the ghats â€” a personal spiritual act.

Taking a Holy Dip

Many pilgrims bathe in the Ganges during Diwali, believing it purifies the soul. If you choose to do this, be respectful and aware of water quality.

Temple Prayers

Attend prayer ceremonies at Kashi Vishwanath or other temples. You don't need to be Hindu to participate â€” approach with respect and genuine spiritual curiosity.

Meditation & Reflection

The quiet hours before sunrise at the ghats are perfect for meditation. Many yogis and spiritual seekers come to Varanasi for this.

Varanasi Diwali Cuisine

Varanasi has its own distinct Diwali food traditions:

  • Malaiyo: A winter specialty dessert made from milk foam (available in winter only).
  • Banarasi Khichdi: A savory rice and lentil dish.
  • Dahi Bhalle: Lentil dumplings in yogurt.
  • Ghugni: Spiced yellow pea curry.
  • Traditional Sweets: Besan ke Laddu, Methai.

Tip: Many restaurants near the ghats cater to tourists. Seek out local homes or street vendors for authentic Varanasi Diwali food.

Safety & Practical Considerations

Health & Hygiene

  • Water quality in the Ganges is concerning. If bathing, don't submerge your head.
  • Carry hand sanitizer and clean your hands frequently.
  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Avoid street food if you have a sensitive stomach.

Crowds & Safety

  • Dev Deepawali draws over 1 million pilgrims. Crowds are massive.
  • Keep valuables secure. Pickpocketing is common in crowded areas.
  • Travel in groups, especially at night.
  • Women should be cautious and consider traveling with companions.

Air Quality

Varanasi's air quality is generally better than Delhi during Diwali, but still can be problematic. Carry masks.

Varanasi Diwali Etiquette

At the Ghats

  • Remove shoes before entering ghat areas (except in shoes-allowed zones).
  • Be respectful during prayers and ceremonies.
  • Photography at cremation ghats (Manikarnika) is absolutely forbidden.
  • Give space to pilgrims performing rituals.

At Temples

  • Remove shoes before entering.
  • Women may need to cover their head at some temples (simple scarf is fine).
  • Don't touch deity idols unless invited.
  • Accept prasad graciously.

General Respect

  • Varanasi is the holiest city in India. Approach with reverence, not as a tourist attraction.
  • Avoid loud conversations, especially near temples and ghats.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly.

Varanasi Diwali FAQ

Is it safe to visit Varanasi during Diwali?

Generally yes, but crowds are extreme. Be aware of pickpockets and stay alert. Avoid dark alleys late at night. The experience is rewarding if you're prepared.

Which is better â€” Main Diwali or Dev Deepawali?

Dev Deepawali is the more iconic Varanasi experience with millions of diyas. If choosing one, pick Dev Deepawali.

Can non-Hindus participate in ceremonies?

Yes, with respect. Approach with genuine spiritual curiosity, not as a spectator. Many temple priests welcome sincere visitors.

Is the Ganges safe to bathe in?

Water quality is poor. Many pilgrims bathe despite this for spiritual reasons. If you choose to, don't submerge your head. Hand and face washing is safer.

What's the best ghat for viewing Dev Deepawali?

Dashaswamedh Ghat is popular but extremely crowded. Assi Ghat or other quieter ghats offer better experiences with fewer crowds.
