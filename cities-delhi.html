Delhi during Diwali is unlike anywhere else on earth. The capital transforms into a glittering celebration of lights, colors, and community. From the ancient lanes of Old Delhi to the modern avenues of New Delhi, the festival brings together millions of people in a shared experience of joy, spirituality, and tradition.

Diwali in Delhi is not just a festival â€” it's a full sensory immersion. The sweet fragrance of diyas and incense fills the air. The sounds of prayers and festivities echo through neighborhoods. The colors of rangoli and lights paint every street.

When to Visit Delhi for Diwali

Diwali 2026: November 6-10, 2026

  • Dhanteras (Nov 6): Markets begin their festive rush. This is when people buy new utensils and gold â€” symbolizing prosperity.
  • Choti Diwali (Nov 7): Smaller celebrations begin. Homes are thoroughly cleaned and decorated.
  • Main Diwali Day (Nov 8): The peak celebration. Lakshmi Puja happens at sunset, followed by fireworks and celebrations through the night.
  • Govardhan Puja (Nov 9): Community celebrations continue with cow worship rituals and family gatherings.
  • Bhai Dooj (Nov 10): The festival concludes with celebrations of sibling bonds.

Best Time to Visit: Arrive 3-4 days before Diwali (Nov 3-4) to experience the market frenzy and preparations. Stay through Nov 10 to experience the full five-day celebration.

Famous Markets & Shopping

Chandni Chowk (Old Delhi)

The legendary market of Old Delhi pulses with energy during Diwali. Narrow lanes overflow with diyas, lights, decorations, sweets, and gift items. The chaos, the crowds, the vendors calling out â€” it's overwhelming and beautiful.

What to expect: Massive crowds, narrow lanes, street food stalls, traditional sweets, lanterns, decorative items.

Pro tip: Go early in the morning or after 8 PM to avoid peak crowd hours. Wear comfortable shoes.

Sarojini Nagar Market

A sprawling wholesale market perfect for gifts, clothing, and decorations at affordable prices. Popular with families and travelers.

CP (Connaught Place)

The upscale shopping hub with boutiques, high-end decorations, and premium gift items. Better for curated shopping without the chaos.

South Delhi Markets (Hauz Khas, Khan Market)

Premium markets with boutique gift items, traditional crafts, and curated Diwali collections.

Where to Stay in Delhi

Old Delhi

Pros: Immersive experience, authentic markets, close to temples and celebrations.

Cons: Congested, noisy, limited accommodations, air quality can be poor.

Neighborhoods: Jama Masjid area, Chandni Chowk lanes.

New Delhi

Pros: Modern amenities, cleaner, more comfortable hotels, metro access.

Cons: Less authentic feel, more expensive.

Neighborhoods: Connaught Place, Karol Bagh, Rajiv Chowk.

South Delhi

Pros: Upscale, quieter, better air quality, safer, more dining options.

Neighborhoods: Hauz Khas, Greater Kailash, Saket.

Important: Book hotels 4-6 months in advance for Diwali. Properties sell out quickly, and rates double or triple.

Diwali Celebrations to Experience

Lakshmi Puja on Diwali Night

If invited to a family's home, the experience of Lakshmi Puja is deeply spiritual. The puja usually begins at sunset. You'll see prayers, offerings of sweets and flowers, the lighting of diyas, and the sense of gratitude and renewal that defines Diwali.

Etiquette: Ask permission to photograph. Sit quietly during prayers. Participate in the distribution of prasad (blessed sweets).

Fireworks Over the City

Diwali night fireworks illuminate Delhi's skyline. Popular viewing spots include India Gate, Rajpath, and rooftop parties in South Delhi.

Note: Air quality can deteriorate significantly due to firecrackers. Wear a mask and stay indoors if AQI exceeds 300.

Rangoli Competitions

Many neighborhoods and housing societies organize rangoli competitions. These colorful floor art displays showcase incredible creativity and traditional design.

Temple Visits

Major temples like Hanuman Temple (Connaught Place), Lakshmi Narayan Temple, and Kali Temple host special Diwali ceremonies and prayers. Visiting temples during Diwali is profound.

Street Celebrations

Walk through neighborhoods in the evenings during Diwali. Every street corner has lights, decorations, and celebrations. The energy is infectious.

Diwali Food & Sweets in Delhi

Delhi's Diwali cuisine is rich and varied. Must-try items include:

  • Gulab Jamun: Soft, sweet milk dough balls in sugar syrup.
  • Jalebi: Orange, sweet spiral snack â€” a Delhi favorite.
  • Barfi: Dense, fudgy sweet made from condensed milk.
  • Kaju Katli: Cashew brittle with a melting texture.
  • Gujhiya: Sweet pastry filled with dry fruits.
  • Mathri: Savory, crispy snack often served with tea.
  • Khir: Rice pudding with cardamom and dry fruits.
  • Samosa: Fried triangular pastry with spiced filling.

Pro tip: Visit renowned sweet shops like Natraj Sweets, Chaina Ram, or Bikanervala to buy authentic Diwali sweets.

Air Quality & Health Considerations

Delhi's air quality is a real concern during Diwali. Firecrackers combined with seasonal crop burning in Punjab and Haryana can create hazardous conditions.

AQI Reality: During Diwali 2025, Delhi's AQI reached 450+ (hazardous level). This is typical. By Nov 9-10, it can spike even higher.

Protective Measures:

  • Carry N95 or N99 masks at all times.
  • Choose hotels with air purifiers and HEPA filters.
  • Limit outdoor activities between 7 PM - 8 AM (peak firecracker hours).
  • Monitor real-time AQI via IQAir app or SAFAR (System of Air Quality and Weather Forecasting).
  • Consider visiting on Nov 6-7 when air is still good, then move to a cleaner city like Jaipur for Nov 8-10.

Health Alert: If you have asthma or respiratory issues, consult your doctor before planning Diwali in Delhi.

Safety & Practical Tips

General Safety

  • Delhi is generally safe during Diwali. Avoid isolated areas late at night.
  • Use registered taxis or Uber/Ola for transportation.
  • Keep valuables secure in crowded markets.
  • Women travelers should avoid solo travel late at night. Travel with trusted locals or organized groups.

Firecracker Safety

  • Keep away from firecrackers â€” injuries are common during Diwali night.
  • Stay indoors or in safe spaces during peak firecracker hours (evening and night).
  • Wear protective gear (gloves, mask) if you venture out.

Traffic & Transportation

  • Traffic in Delhi is extremely congested during Diwali week.
  • Book hotel/airport transfers in advance.
  • Use Delhi Metro for reliable, uncongested transportation.
  • Plan travel times with 2x the usual duration.

Diwali Etiquette in Delhi

What to Wear

Bright, festive colors are highly appreciated. Traditional Indian clothing (kurta, saree, salwar kameez) is especially welcomed and shows respect. If you wear Western clothing, opt for bright colors and smart casual attire. Avoid black and white.

Gifts to Bring

When visiting someone's home, bring:

  • Boxes of high-quality Indian sweets (mithai)
  • Dry fruits (almonds, cashews)
  • Decorative diyas or candles
  • Chocolate hampers
  • Silver coins (symbolize prosperity)

Home Visits

  • Always remove shoes before entering a home.
  • Compliment the rangoli at the entrance.
  • Accept sweets and prasad graciously.
  • Participate in prayers if invited, but it's okay to simply sit quietly if you prefer.
  • Be respectful during Lakshmi Puja â€” avoid loud conversations.

Photography

  • Always ask before photographing family members or religious ceremonies.
  • Never photograph someone praying without permission.
  • Public streets and markets are generally fine to photograph.

Sample 5-Day Delhi Diwali Itinerary

Nov 6 (Dhanteras)

  • Arrive in Delhi, check into hotel.
  • Explore Chandni Chowk market in the morning (less crowded).
  • Visit Hanuman Temple or Lakshmi Narayan Temple.
  • Evening: Wander through lit neighborhoods, enjoy street food.

Nov 7 (Choti Diwali)

  • Breakfast at a local cafe.
  • Visit Jama Masjid and surrounding Old Delhi.
  • Afternoon: Shopping for gifts and decorations.
  • Evening: Enjoy a family dinner with local cuisine.

Nov 8 (Main Diwali)

  • If invited: Participate in a family Lakshmi Puja (5-7 PM).
  • Early dinner at home or restaurant.
  • Evening: Watch the city light up, view fireworks from a safe location.
  • Optional: Hotel rooftop celebration.

Nov 9 (Govardhan Puja)

  • Visit a temple for Govardhan Puja ceremonies.
  • Explore South Delhi (Hauz Khas, Delhi Zoo area).
  • Enjoy afternoon rangoli walks in residential areas.
  • Evening: Relax, prepare to leave Delhi if air quality is poor.

Nov 10 (Bhai Dooj)

  • Final market visits or sightseeing.
  • Farewell meal with local friends or hotel staff.
  • Depart in the afternoon.

Delhi Diwali FAQ

Is it safe to travel to Delhi during Diwali?

Yes, Delhi is generally safe for travelers during Diwali. However, be prepared for crowds, congestion, and air quality issues. Avoid isolated areas late at night, use registered transportation, and follow local guidance on air quality.

How bad is the air quality in Delhi during Diwali?

Air quality can reach hazardous levels (AQI 400+) on Diwali night and the following days due to firecrackers and seasonal crop burning. If you have respiratory issues, consider visiting another city or staying indoors with air purifiers.

What's the best hotel area to stay in Delhi?

For first-time visitors seeking comfort, New Delhi or South Delhi are best. For immersive experience, stay in or near Old Delhi (Jama Masjid area). Book 4-6 months in advance.

Can I visit markets on Diwali night itself?

Most markets close or wind down by evening on Diwali night as business owners go home to celebrate with family. Visit markets on Nov 6-7 for the full shopping experience.

Do I need to be Hindu to participate in Diwali celebrations?

Absolutely not. Diwali is a festival of light celebrated by people of all faiths. Visitors and non-Hindus are warmly welcomed to participate respectfully.
