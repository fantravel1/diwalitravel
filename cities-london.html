Trafalgar Square Diwali Festival

The Main Event

London's Trafalgar Square hosts the UK's largest Diwali celebration. Usually held on a Sunday close to Diwali, it attracts 70,000-100,000 people. It's free, outdoor, and spectacular.

What to Expect

  • Performances: Bollywood dance, classical Indian dance, fusion performances
  • Food stalls: Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi cuisine
  • Art installations: Contemporary and traditional art
  • Market stalls: Indian clothing, jewelry, crafts
  • Fireworks display: Evening finale (when applicable)
  • Community: Incredibly diverse, welcoming, celebratory atmosphere

Practical Information

  • Date: Usually first Sunday in November (Nov 2, 2026)
  • Time: 12 PM - 7 PM typically
  • Entry: FREE
  • Location: Trafalgar Square (central London, easy access)
  • Transport: Multiple Underground stations nearby

Tips for the Festival

  • Arrive early (before 1 PM) for parking and crowds
  • Bring cash (many vendors don't have card readers)
  • Wear warm layers (November in London is cool)
  • Comfortable walking shoes essential
  • Plan 3-4 hours for full experience

Temple & Spiritual Sites

Neasden Hindu Temple

Europe's largest Hindu temple. Stunning architecture, active spiritual community, special Diwali celebrations with lighting displays.

  • Location: Neasden, Northwest London
  • During Diwali: Extended hours, special ceremonies, thousands of lights
  • Entry: Free (donations appreciated)

Sri Guru Singh Sabha Gurdwara

Major Sikh temple in Southall. Active Sikh community, Diwali/Bandi Chhor Divas celebrations, langar meals available.

Brick Lane Mosques & Bengali Temples

East London's historic South Asian neighborhood. Multiple temples, mosques, and cultural sites. Vibrant street celebrations.

Buddhist Lodge & Other Temples

London has temples from all South Asian religions and sects. Many hold special Diwali events. Check local listings.

Diwali Markets & Shopping

Where to Shop

  • Southall Broadway: Traditional Indian clothing, jewelry, sweets, home goods
  • Brick Lane: Bangladeshi and Indian clothing, spices, crafts
  • West End: Modern Indian/fusion fashion stores
  • Asian supermarkets: Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda all stock Diwali items

What to Buy

  • Indian clothing (salwar, saris, kurtas)
  • Traditional sweets and dry fruits
  • Decorative items and lamps
  • Spices and cooking ingredients
  • Jewelry and accessories

Timing

Shop early November (first week) for best selection. Prices increase closer to Diwali date.

Diwali Food in London

Traditional Sweets & Dry Fruits

  • Laddu: Various types available
  • Barfi: Milk-based fudge in many varieties
  • Jalebis: Syrup-soaked spiral sweets
  • Dry fruits: Almonds, pistachios, cashews, dates

Where to Eat

  • Southall restaurants: Most authentic Punjabi/North Indian
  • Brick Lane curry houses: Bangladeshi specialties
  • Fine dining: Upscale Indian restaurants across London
  • Street food: Samosas, pakoras, chaat at festivals

Sweet Shops

  • Southall has numerous traditional mithai shops
  • Online Indian sweet delivery (Soho Sweets, Haandi, etc.)
  • Asian supermarkets with sweet sections

5-Day London Diwali Itinerary

Nov 2 (Trafalgar Square Festival Day)

  • Morning: Explore central London
  • Afternoon: Head to Trafalgar Square (arrive early for parking/crowds)
